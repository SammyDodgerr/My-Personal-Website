I'm Tristan

I am looking for my first role as a Front-end Web Developer, so I have created a 'Personal Project' website. My favourite hobby is drawing, so I have displayed some of my most recent and pen and pencil works.

My typical subject matter is the landscapes and building around me in the United Kingdom. Having lived in the country all my life, I enjoy replicating scenes I have seen from memory and photographs alike.

Depicting buildings is enjoyable, because I can demonstrate perspective based upon the angle and viewpoint. The details such as brickwork, lighting or pipe work are recognisable and therefore pleasing to replicate.

Furthermore, drawing 'The Den', home to Millwall Football Club intriguing, because of the physical size of the stadium and its significance to the local community in Bermondsey, South London. Football Stadiums have a particular prominence in the urban typography of towns and cities in Britain. They can be viewed as landmarks; juxtaposed to housing estates, shopping parades and pubs.

Working with fine line pens is useful for attempting to reimagine British scenes, because the dark tones can be utilised to show the gloomy weather!

For similar reasons to my drawing of a stadium, I chose to draw a windmill close to where I grew up. Standing on the top of hills in rural landscapes, windmills can be uncommon features, sharply contrasting the farmland, roads and villages surrounding them.

Drawing brickwork is the best part of creating building themed artwork in my experience. It can simultaneously provide scale, curvature and shadow; bringing previously flat looking objects to life and (hopefully) showing an effective illusion of 3-dimension.

Blades of grass and knots in the wood of fencing and gates are other intricate details, contributing to an overall image and sense of realism.
